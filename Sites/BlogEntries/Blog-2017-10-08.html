Software Engineering - Week 6 (Oct. 2 - Oct. 6)

And welcome to week six of the Software Engineering course! This week saw little work from this class specifically, but that has a good reason. Next week, we are having our first exam, and I am guessing that has something to do with the low amount of coursework.

Speculations aside, it is time to get to these questions!

What did you do this past week?

In the class we learned more about iterables, generators, iterators (and how they ought to implement the __iter__() and __next() methods), and studied the "yield" keyword in Python. This is all really neat (especially the "yield" keyword), and I hope to get a chance to use it in one form or another.

What's in your way?

I believe this will become a trend as this semester progresses, but my other classes are getting in the way. No matter how many times I estimate the time that other class projects will take, I am always surprised by new developments. I have heard it said, "Plans are worthless, but planning is everything." It might be Dwight D. Eisenhower who said it. Regardless, I can not agree with this more as the semester progresses.

What will you do next week?

I will take some time to study for the first exam. Other than that, I plan to work on projects for other classes; be a good student, basically.

What's my experience in the class?

My experience is the same as ever. I like the style of teaching and the general pace of it all. I also appreciate Professor Downing's willingness to repeat information. It really helps when it comes to remembering any concept, really.

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

Be a good student! Work hard, study hard, and rest whenever you have the chance.


That is all for this week. Have a good one!
