Software Engineering - Week 9 (Oct. 23 - Oct. 27)

Hello and welcome to the ninth-week review of the Software Engineering course!

So, this week we stopped learning about Python-specific topics and moved on to SQL lessons! I believe all those who read this blog will know enough of programming languages to know what SQL is, but, for those who do not know, SQL is a language built specifically for data retrieval from a database. It was definitely a nice change of topics!

Anyway, enough with the review. On to the questions and answers!


What did you do this past week?

This past week saw the completion of phase two of the internet database project and, as stated above, the introduction of a new programming language! The second phase was a rough one. We had to split our team into two: frontend and backend. This is the first time, alongside my other class this semester, that I have ever had only a subset of my team that I could count on for immediate help, so this was something!

What's in your way?

As said before, my other classes are in the way, and I can't do a thing 'bout that. C'est la vie, unfortunately.

What will you do next week?

I will try to catch up with my other classes now that this hectic phase is done with, but I have a feeling phase three will not be any easier. Anyway, I will also try to keep up with the reading and do well on the quizzes.

What's my experience in the class?

My opinion is the same as ever: I like the way Professor Downing teaches, but the lectures have little, if anything, to do with the projects themselves. It is kind of a drag, but I do see how this prepares us for the future. Except, ya' know, I will not have to attend school anymore, and I will have a ton of free time. (Just so you all know, I have a thing against school. Not education. Just school.)

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

Keep at it. This is tough, but keep at it. This will pass. And, hey, if you are a senior, have a good GPA, and need one or two more semesters to graduate, remember what one of my senior team members once said: "C's get degrees." Now, obviously, try your best and do not be a drag to your team, but you are in a good spot. Smile. c:


I hope you are all enjoying this! See you all next time!
