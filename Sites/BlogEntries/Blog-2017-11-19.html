Software Engineering - Week 12 (Nov. 13 - Nov. 17)

Hello and welcome to the review of the twelfth week of the Software Engineering course!

Unfortunately, I missed both Monday and Friday of this week, so some answers will not be as detailed as others. Sorry for the inconvenience

Moving right along, the questions need some answers!


What did you do this past week?

This past week, we learned about method overriding, dynamic binding, the singleton pattern, and reflection. These were all in the Java language, of course. Sadly, these are the lessons that I missed, so I have a bit of work to do to catch up. The class I did not miss, however, was the class on ethics in for software engineers. Though not comprehensive, this lecture highlighted the importance of morality in coding.

What's in your way?

For the rest of the semester, the things that will stand in my way will be my other classes. There is no changing that, I am afraid.

What will you do next week?

Next week is the start of the Thanksgiving break! As such, this will be a short week with only one day of lecture for this class. I do not know what next week's only lecture will be about, so I do not know what I will do. Let us wait and see!

What's my experience in the class?

My opinion still has not changed: Professor Downing's style of teaching is still great, still tangentially related to the projects, and still engaging. Nothing new to report on this front.

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

Take care of your health! The reason I missed Friday's lecture is because I got sick again. People keep thinking it is okay to work in close proximity to others while coughing up a storm. People will, sadly, keep doing this, so do take care of yourself and ask them to cover themselves well when coughing -- a simple hand-to-mouth motion will not cut it.


That is all for this entry! See you all next time!
